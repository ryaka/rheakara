Notes - Deep Speech 2: End-to-End Speech Recognition in English and Mandarin

  • “We decided to examine GRUs because experiments on smaller data sets showed the GRU and LSTM reach similar accuracy for the same number of parameters, but the GRUs were faster to train and less likely to diverge”
  • “the performance improvement from sequence-wise (batch) normalization increases with the depth of the network, with a 12% performance difference for the deepest network”
  • ” This is clear evidence of the long term dependencies inherent in the speech recognition task present both within individual words and between words”
  • “The GRU networks outperform the simple RNNs in Table 3. However, in later results (Section 6) we find that as we scale up the model size, for a fixed computational budget the simple RNN networks perform slightly better. Given this, most of the remaining experiments use the simple RNN layers rather than the GRUs.” !!!!
  • “striding can reduce accuracy simply because the output of our network requires at least one timestep per output character, and the number of characters in English speech per time-step is high enough to cause problems when striding2 . To overcome this, we can enrich the English alphabet with symbols representing alternate labellings like whole words, syllables or non-overlapping ngrams”

However in when trying to use in online, low latency manner, must be able to operate on sample without requiring entire utterance…successful getting around this unidirectionally with usage of “ a special layer that we call row convolution”

  • “We have found that deploying our models using half-precision (16-bit) floating-point arithmetic does not measurably change recognition accuracy. Because deployment does not require any updates to the network weights, it is far less sensitive to numerical precision than training”
